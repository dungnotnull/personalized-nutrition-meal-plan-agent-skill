# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and collaborative environment for all contributors to the Personalized Nutrition & Meal Plan project. We prioritize user safety, evidence-based practice, and respectful discourse.

---

## Our Standards

### Inclusive Language

We encourage language that:
- Welcomes diverse perspectives and experiences
- Respects all body types, abilities, and health conditions
- Avoids stigmatizing language about weight, eating, or health
- Uses person-first language (e.g., "person with diabetes" not "diabetic")
- Respects cultural food traditions and patterns

### Examples of Positive Conduct

- **Respectful disagreement**: "I see this differently. Here's my perspective based on [evidence]..."
- **Evidence-based discussions**: Citing authoritative sources for claims
- **Safety-conscious**: Always considering user wellbeing first
- **Inclusive**: Welcoming contributors from diverse backgrounds
- **Constructive**: Focused on improving the project for all users

### Examples of Unacceptable Conduct

- **Weight stigma**: Negative language about body size or weight
- **Food policing**: Judging others' food choices or cultural practices
- **Medical advice**: Prescriptive language outside professional scope
- **Harassment**: Personal attacks, disrespectful language, or dismissal
- **Exclusion**: Marginalizing any group or individual
- **Safety violations**: Suggesting bypass of safety protocols

---

## User Safety Paramount

### Non-Negotiable Principles

1. **Never bypass safety screening** — The safety screener exists to protect users. Suggesting ways to circumvent it is unacceptable.

2. **Never provide medical advice** — We provide educational information only. Conditions requiring professional care must be referred out.

3. **Never stigmatize** — Language that shames users about their bodies, eating, or health conditions has no place here.

4. **Always prioritize evidence** — Claims must be supported by authoritative sources. Anecdotes alone are insufficient.

### Safety-Related Reporting

If you observe content that could harm users:
- **Immediately flag** to maintainers
- **Do not merge** potentially unsafe changes
- **Provide context** about safety concerns

---

## Responsibilities

### Contributors

- Ensure safety screening is never compromised
- Base contributions on authoritative evidence
- Use inclusive, respectful language
- Welcome feedback from diverse perspectives
- Credit sources appropriately

### Maintainers

- Review all contributions for safety and accuracy
- Ensure referral resources are current
- Model respectful and evidence-based discourse
- Address conduct issues promptly and fairly
- Document decisions transparently

### Users

- Use the skill for educational purposes only
- Consult professionals for personalized care
- Report issues or concerns constructively
- Respect the educational (not prescriptive) nature of output

---

## Reporting Issues

### Conduct Concerns

If you experience or witness unacceptable conduct:

1. **Contact maintainers** directly (see below)
2. **Describe the issue** clearly with context
3. **Provide evidence** if applicable (screenshots, links)
4. **Expect confidentiality** — Reports handled privately

### Safety Concerns

If you identify a potential safety issue (e.g., bypassed screening, incorrect referral):

1. **Flag immediately** — Do not wait for normal process
2. **Label clearly**: "SAFETY CONCERN" in subject
3. **Describe risk**: What could happen to users
4. **Suggest fix**: If possible, recommend remediation

## Enforcement

### Process

1. **Report received** — Acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Investigation** — Maintainers review evidence and context
3. **Resolution** — Appropriate action determined
4. **Follow-up** — Ensures issue is resolved and doesn't recur

### Possible Actions

| Severity | Action | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Minor | Private warning | Clarify expectations, educate |
| Moderate | Public correction | Address issue transparently |
| Severe | Temporary suspension | Restrict access for defined period |
| Critical | Permanent ban | Remove from project entirely |

### Appeals

- Appeals may be submitted to [backup maintainer]
- Must include new evidence or context
- Reviewed by different maintainer if possible
- Decision within 7 days

## Additional Principles

### Evidence-Based Discourse

- **Cite sources** for claims about nutrition or health
- **Distinguish evidence tiers** — Systematic review > RCT > observational
- **Acknowledge uncertainty** — Don't overstate evidence
- **Update positions** — When new evidence emerges, adapt guidance

### Cultural Humility

- **Respect food traditions** — Cultural food patterns are valid
- **Avoid gatekeeping** — No single "correct" way to eat
- **Learn from users** — Cultural practices enrich our understanding
- **Challenge biases** — Examine Eurocentric assumptions

### Accessibility

- **Clear language** — Avoid unnecessary jargon
- **Plain language options** — Make content understandable to all
- **Multiple formats** — Consider visual, audio, simplified versions
- **User testing** — Include diverse users in testing

## Commitment

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to prioritize user safety, maintain evidence-based standards, and foster inclusive collaboration.
